History of Arellano University

Arellano University, founded in 1938 by Florentino Cayco, Sr. and Carmen de Luna Cayco, began as the Arellano Law College named after Cayetano Arellano, the first Chief Justice of the Supreme Court of the Philippines.

Initially offering legal education, the university expanded its academic repertoire over time, encompassing fields such as education, commerce, and engineering. With campuses across Metro Manila and other provinces, including its main campus in Sampaloc, Manila, Arellano University has earned recognition for its quality programs and received accreditation both nationally and internationally.

Renowned for its sports programs, particularly basketball, the university's teams have excelled in various collegiate leagues. Arellano University continues to evolve, embracing modernization, expanding its academic offerings, and maintaining its commitment to providing accessible and high-quality education, leaving a significant legacy in Philippine education with its distinguished alumni contributing to diverse fields.
